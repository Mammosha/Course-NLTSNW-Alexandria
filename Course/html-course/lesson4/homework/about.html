George Orwell 25.06.1903-21.01.1950

 Artur Blair, known by his pen name George Orwell, was English novelist, essayist, journalist and critic.[1] His work is characterised by lucid prose, biting social criticism, total opposition to totalitarism, and outspoken support of democratic socialism.

 Nineteen Eighty-Four (also stylised as 1984) is a dystopian social science fiction novel and cautionary tale written by English writer George Orwell
